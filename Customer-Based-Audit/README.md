This project focuses on analyzing the customer base of KazMarket using transactional data. The goal of the analysis is to understand how customers differ in terms of spending behavior, transaction frequency, and revenue contribution. Using methods such as decile analysis, cohort analysis, and yearly summaries, the study evaluates customer profitability, the concentration of revenue across customer segments, and the overall health of the company’s customer base. The analysis also identifies key strengths and weaknesses in the customer structure and evaluates the company’s potential for future growth based on observed customer behavior patterns.

This project helped develop several analytical and technical skills related to customer data analysis. First, I worked with transactional datasets in Excel and learned how to organize and clean data in order to perform meaningful analysis. I applied quantitative analytical methods such as decile analysis, cohort analysis, and yearly summaries to evaluate customer behavior and segment the customer base according to value. These methods allowed me to identify differences in customer profitability, purchasing frequency, and revenue contribution across customer groups.

In addition, I strengthened my data visualization skills by creating clear tables and diagrams to communicate analytical results effectively. Using charts such as revenue by decile and transaction distribution, I learned how to present insights in a way that supports data-driven decision making. The project also improved my ability to interpret numerical results and translate them into business insights about customer retention, concentration of revenue, and potential growth opportunities.

Overall, this assignment enhanced my skills in data analysis, Excel-based modeling, interpretation of transactional data, and communication of analytical findings in a structured and professional business report.
