This project focused on analysing the risk and return characteristics of two major entertainment companies, Netflix (NFLX) and The Walt Disney Company (DIS). Weekly price data was collected and converted into returns in order to evaluate the performance of both assets. The analysis applied several statistical tools commonly used in finance, including descriptive statistics, variance and standard deviation, the coefficient of variation, covariance, and the Sharpe ratio. These measures allowed for a comparison of the two stocks in terms of both profitability and investment risk, providing insight into which asset offers a more favourable risk–return profile.

During this exercise I developed practical skills in financial data analysis using spreadsheet tools. I learned how to collect and organize financial data from external sources and prepare it for statistical analysis. I calculated weekly arithmetic returns from historical price data and applied descriptive statistics such as mean, median, variance, and standard deviation to evaluate the distribution and volatility of returns.

In addition, I gained experience comparing investments using risk–return measures such as the coefficient of variation and the Sharpe ratio. These metrics allowed me to assess not only the potential profitability of the assets but also the level of risk associated with achieving those returns. I also improved my ability to interpret statistical results and explain them in the context of investment decision-making.

Finally, this project strengthened my skills in data organization and presentation within spreadsheets, including structuring datasets, applying formulas, and presenting results in a clear and professional format suitable for analytical reports or professional portfolios.
